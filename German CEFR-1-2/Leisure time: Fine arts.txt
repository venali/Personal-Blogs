1) Einführung: Bildende Kunst in der Freizeit
English translation (sentence-by-sentence):
Fine arts enrich leisure by combining aesthetic experiences with reflection and dialogue. At C1 level, we describe exhibitions precisely, express nuanced evaluations, and structure recommendations in formal and informal registers.
German (with glosses):
Die (the) bildenden (fine/visual) Künste (arts) bereichern (enrich) die (the) Freizeit (leisure) durch (through) die (the) Verbindung (combination) von (of) ästhetischen (aesthetic) Erlebnissen (experiences) mit (with) Reflexion (reflection) und (and) Dialog (dialogue).
Auf (at) C1-Niveau (C1-level) beschreiben (describe) wir (we) Ausstellungen (exhibitions) präzise (precisely), äußern (express) nuancierte (nuanced) Bewertungen (evaluations), und (and) strukturieren (structure) Empfehlungen (recommendations) in (in) formellen (formal) und (and) informellen (informal) Registern (registers).

2) Präpositionen und Ortsangaben
English translation:
We use “in” for being inside institutions, “an” for referencing specific works or stations, “auf” for surfaces or platforms, “bei” for events, “zwischen” for spatial relations, “gegenüber” for facing positions, and “entlang” for movement along displays.
German (glosses):
Wir (we) verwenden (use) in (in) für (for) das (the) Befinden (being) im (in-the) Museum (museum) oder (or) in (in) der (the) Galerie (gallery), an (at) für (for) den (the) Bezug (reference) an (at) einem (a) Werk (work), auf (on) für (for) Flächen (surfaces) oder (or) Plattformen (platforms), bei (at) für (for) Veranstaltungen (events), zwischen (between) für (for) räumliche (spatial) Beziehungen (relations), gegenüber (opposite) für (for) gegenüberliegende (facing) Positionen (positions), und (and) entlang (along) für (for) Bewegung (movement) entlang (along) der (the) Exponate (exhibits).
English translation (examples):
We are in the gallery. She stands at the sculpture. The installation lies on the platform. The lecture takes place at the museum. The paintings hang between the windows. The new work is opposite the entrance. We walk along the exhibition.
German (glosses):
Wir (we) sind (are) in (in) der (the) Galerie (gallery).
Sie (she) steht (stands) an (at) der (the) Skulptur (sculpture).
Die (the) Installation (installation) liegt (lies) auf (on) der (the) Plattform (platform).
Die (the) Vorlesung (lecture) findet (takes-place) bei (at) dem (the) Museum (museum) statt (takes-place).
Die (the) Gemälde (paintings) hängen (hang) zwischen (between) den (the) Fenstern (windows).
Das (the) neue (new) Werk (work) ist (is) gegenüber (opposite) dem (the) Eingang (entrance).
Wir (we) gehen (walk) entlang (along) der (the) Ausstellung (exhibition).

3) Tempus & Aspekt: Kontrastierte Zeitformen in Kulturkontexten
English translation:
Present describes viewing and general truths; perfect is common in spoken past; simple past is usual in written reviews; past perfect marks earlier past; Futur I/II express plans and completion in the future.
German (glosses):
Das (the) Präsens (present) beschreibt (describes) das (the) Betrachten (viewing) und (and) allgemeine (general) Wahrheiten (truths); das (the) Perfekt (perfect) ist (is) in (in) gesprochener (spoken) Vergangenheit (past) üblich (usual); das (the) Präteritum (simple-past) ist (is) in (in) schriftlichen (written) Kritiken (reviews) üblich (common); das (the) Plusquamperfekt (past-perfect) markiert (marks) eine (an) frühere (earlier) Vergangenheit (past); Futur (future) I (I) / II (II) drücken (express) Pläne (plans) und (and) Abgeschlossenheit (completion) in (in) der (the) Zukunft (future) aus (out).
Examples (with sentence-by-sentence translations):
English: Today, we view the retrospective; yesterday, I visited the vernissage; last year, the museum curated a major show; before I arrived, she had already seen the installation; tomorrow we will attend the talk; by next week, they will have published the catalogue.
German (glosses):
Heute (today) betrachten (view) wir (we) die (the) Retrospektive (retrospective).
Gestern (yesterday) habe (have) ich (I) die (the) Vernissage (opening) besucht (visited).
Letztes (last) Jahr (year) kuratierte (curated) das (the) Museum (museum) eine (a) große (major) Schau (show).
Bevor (before) ich (I) ankam (arrived), hatte (had) sie (she) die (the) Installation (installation) bereits (already) gesehen (seen).
Morgen (tomorrow) werden (will) wir (we) den (the) Vortrag (talk) besuchen (attend).
Bis (by) nächste (next) Woche (week) werden (will) sie (they) den (the) Katalog (catalogue) veröffentlicht (published) haben (have).

4) Modalität & Nuancen
English translation:
Modals shape recommendations and rules: “should” for advice, “must” for rules, “may” for permission, “can” for capability, “would like” for polite wishes, “will” to express assumption.
German (glosses):
Modalverben (modal-verbs) formen (shape) Empfehlungen (recommendations) und (and) Regeln (rules): sollen (should) für (for) Ratschläge (advice), müssen (must) für (for) Regeln (rules), dürfen (may) für (for) Erlaubnis (permission), können (can) für (for) Fähigkeit (capability), möchten (would-like) für (for) höfliche (polite) Wünsche (wishes), werden (will/probably) für (for) Annahmen (assumptions).
English translation (examples):
You should visit the sculpture courtyard. Visitors must not touch the paintings. You may take photos without flash. We can book the guided tour online. I would like to attend the curator’s talk. The gallery will probably be crowded at six.
German (glosses):
Sie (you) sollten (should) den (the) Skulpturenhof (sculpture-courtyard) besuchen (visit).
Besucher (visitors) dürfen (may) die (the) Gemälde (paintings) nicht (not) berühren (touch).
Sie (you) dürfen (may) ohne (without) Blitz (flash) fotografieren (take-photos).
Wir (we) können (can) die (the) Führung (guided-tour) online (online) buchen (book).
Ich (I) möchte (would-like) dem (the) Vortrag (talk) der (the) Kuratorin (curator-fem.) beiwohnen (attend).
Die (the) Galerie (gallery) wird (will/probably) um (at) sechs (six) Uhr (o’clock) überfüllt (crowded) sein (be).

5) Komplexe Strukturen: Relativsätze, Partizipialattribute, Konjunktiv II
English translation:
Relative clauses add precision; participial attributes condense information; Konjunktiv II softens requests and recommendations.
German (glosses):
Relativsätze (relative-clauses) fügen (add) Präzision (precision) hinzu (to):
Das (the) Werk (work), das (which) 1923 (1923) entstanden (created) ist (is), zeigt (shows) eine (a) radikale (radical) Komposition (composition).
Partizipialattribute (participial-attributes) verdichten (condense) Information (information):
Die (the) vom (by-the) Museum (museum) kuratierte (curated) Ausstellung (exhibition) begeistert (delights) das (the) Publikum (audience).
Konjunktiv (subjunctive) II (II) mildert (softens) Bitten (requests):
Es (it) wäre (would-be) sehr (very) freundlich (kind), wenn (if) Sie (you) die (the) Provenienz (provenance) erläutern (explain) könnten (could).
English translation (example sentence):
The painting, which was acquired last year, hangs opposite the entrance; the newly restored sculpture attracts many visitors; it would be helpful if you could recommend a catalogue.
German (glosses):
Das (the) Gemälde (painting), das (which) letztes (last) Jahr (year) erworben (acquired) wurde (was), hängt (hangs) gegenüber (opposite) dem (the) Eingang (entrance);
die (the) neu (newly) restaurierte (restored) Skulptur (sculpture) zieht (attracts) viele (many) Besucher (visitors) an (to);
es (it) wäre (would-be) hilfreich (helpful), wenn (if) Sie (you) einen (a) Katalog (catalogue) empfehlen (recommend) könnten (could).

6) Adverbiale Präzision
English translation:
Adverbs refine time and stance: already, still, only just, just now, further, simultaneously, explicitly.
German (glosses):
Adverbien (adverbs) verfeinern (refine) Zeit (time) und (and) Haltung (stance): bereits (already), noch (still), erst (only-just), gerade (just-now), weiterhin (further/still), zugleich (simultaneously), ausdrücklich (explicitly).
English translation (examples):
We have already seen the portraits. She is still in the sculpture hall. I have only just arrived. The curator is just now speaking. The exhibition remains further open. The installation is simultaneously immersive and reflective. The notice explicitly forbids touching.
German (glosses):
Wir (we) haben (have) die (the) Porträts (portraits) bereits (already) gesehen (seen).
Sie (she) ist (is) noch (still) in (in) der (the) Skulpturenhalle (sculpture-hall).
Ich (I) bin (am) erst (only-just) angekommen (arrived).
Die (the) Kuratorin (curator-fem.) spricht (speaks) gerade (just-now).
Die (the) Ausstellung (exhibition) bleibt (remains) weiterhin (further/still) offen (open).
Die (the) Installation (installation) ist (is) zugleich (simultaneously) immersiv (immersive) und (and) reflektiv (reflective).
Der (the) Hinweis (notice) verbietet (forbids) ausdrücklich (explicitly) das (the) Berühren (touching).

8) Mini-Dialoge & kurze Texte
8.1 Planung einer Galerie-Tour
English translation (sentence-by-sentence):
A: Tomorrow at eleven, shall we visit the fine arts gallery?
B: Yes, we should start with the sculpture courtyard and then view the retrospective.
A: Good plan—after we have bought the tickets, we will check the catalogue.
German (glosses):
A: Morgen (tomorrow) um (at) elf (eleven) wollen (shall) wir (we) die (the) Galerie (gallery) der (of-the) bildenden (fine/visual) Künste (arts) besuchen (visit)?
B: Ja (yes), wir (we) sollten (should) mit (with) dem (the) Skulpturenhof (sculpture-courtyard) beginnen (begin) und (and) dann (then) die (the) Retrospektive (retrospective) ansehen (view).
A: Guter (good) Plan (plan)—nachdem (after) wir (we) die (the) Karten (tickets) gekauft (bought) haben (have), werden (will) wir (we) den (the) Katalog (catalogue) prüfen (check).

8.2 Betrachtung eines Gemäldes
English translation:
A: The composition guides the eye diagonally.
B: The texture creates a palpable depth; it is both abstract and expressive.
A: Could you explain the provenance?
B: It would be helpful if we read the wall label first.
German (glosses):
A: Die (the) Komposition (composition) lenkt (guides) den (the) Blick (eye) diagonal (diagonally).
B: Die (the) Textur (texture) erzeugt (creates) eine (a) spürbare (palpable) Tiefe (depth); sie (it) ist (is) zugleich (simultaneously) abstrakt (abstract) und (and) expressiv (expressive).
A: Könnten (could) Sie (you) die (the) Provenienz (provenance) erläutern (explain)?
B: Es (it) wäre (would-be) hilfreich (helpful), wenn (if) wir (we) zuerst (first) das (the) Wandetikett (wall-label) lesen (read) würden (would).

8.3 Kurze Kritik (C1-Stil)
English translation:
The newly curated exhibition challenges conventions by juxtaposing mediums and eras. While the installation design is coherent, the lighting appears overly stark; however, the catalogue compensates with insightful essays.
German (glosses):
Die (the) neu (newly) kuratierte (curated) Ausstellung (exhibition) stellt (challenges/places) Konventionen (conventions) infrage (into-question), indem (by) sie (it) Medien (media) und (and) Epochen (eras) nebeneinanderstellt (juxtaposes).
Während (while) das (the) Installationsdesign (installation-design) stimmig (coherent) ist (is), wirkt (appears) die (the) Beleuchtung (lighting) übermäßig (overly) hart (stark); jedoch (however) gleicht (compensates) der (the) Katalog (catalogue) dies (this) durch (through) aufschlussreiche (insightful) Essays (essays) aus (out).

10) Zusammenfassung & Praxistext
English translation:
At C1 level, speaking about fine arts requires precise spatial prepositions, flexible tense use for narrative and critique, modal verbs for permissions and recommendations, complex structures like relative clauses and participial attributes, and adverbs to capture nuance. Formal politeness with Konjunktiv II ensures respectful communication in galleries and museums.
German (glosses):
Auf (at) C1-Niveau (C1-level) erfordert (requires) das (the) Sprechen (speaking) über (about) bildende (fine/visual) Kunst (art) präzise (precise) räumliche (spatial) Präpositionen (prepositions), flexiblen (flexible) Tempusgebrauch (use-of-tense) für (for) Erzählung (narrative) und (and) Kritik (critique), Modalverben (modal-verbs) für (for) Erlaubnis (permission) und (and) Empfehlung (recommendation), komplexe (complex) Strukturen (structures) wie (like) Relativsätze (relative-clauses) und (and) Partizipialattribute (participial-attributes), sowie (as-well-as) Adverbien (adverbs) zur (to-the) Nuancierung (nuancing).
Höfliche (polite) Formulierungen (formulations) mit (with) dem (the) Konjunktiv (subjunctive) II (II) sichern (ensure) respektvolle (respectful) Kommunikation (communication) in (in) Galerien (galleries) und (and) Museen (museums).

By next week, the catalogue will have been published; in the meantime, the gallery remains open.

Extra: Stilmittel in Kunstkritik (kurz)
English translation:
Use contrast markers (“however”), causal links (“because”), concessive (“although”), and participial compression (“newly restored sculpture”) to create analytic flow.
German (glosses):
Nutze (use) Kontrastmarker (contrast-markers) (jedoch (however)), kausale (causal) Verknüpfungen (links) (weil (because)), konzessive (concessive) (obwohl (although)), und (and) partizipiale (participial) Verdichtung (compression) (neu (newly) restaurierte (restored) Skulptur (sculpture)), um (to) einen (an) analytischen (analytic) Fluss (flow) zu (to) erzeugen (create).
